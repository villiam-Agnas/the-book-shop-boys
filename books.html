Books i have read this year so far

The Fellowship Of The Ring

The Fellowship of the Ring, the first volume of The Lord of the Rings trilogy, introduces readers to the richly imagined world of Middle-earth—a land of ancient histories, diverse peoples, and powerful forces, both light and dark. The story centers on a seemingly ordinary hobbit named Frodo Baggins who finds himself entrusted with a mysterious and powerful object. Though small in stature and far from the affairs of kings and warriors, Frodo becomes the unlikely bearer of a burden that could determine the fate of the entire world.As darkness rises in the East and rumors of evil spread, Frodo sets out on a perilous journey beyond his quiet homeland. He is not alone. Joined by an unlikely group of companions drawn from different races and cultures—hobbits, men, an elf, a dwarf, and a wizard—Frodo forms a fellowship united by a common goal. Together, they must navigate treacherous lands, face ancient threats, and confront the temptation and danger that the object they carry presents.Tolkien’s masterful storytelling blends myth, adventure, friendship, and moral conflict into a tale that feels both timeless and epic. The Fellowship of the Ring explores themes of courage, loyalty, and the burden of responsibility in the face of overwhelming odds. With lyrical prose and deep world-building, it lays the foundation for a grand tale of heroism and sacrifice that continues to captivate readers across generations.

The Two Towers

The Two Towers, the second volume of The Lord of the Rings trilogy, continues the epic journey through the vast and perilous world of Middle-earth. Following the separation of the Fellowship at the end of The Fellowship of the Ring, the narrative splits into two distinct paths, both of which are fraught with danger and uncertainty.In one thread, Frodo Baggins and his loyal companion, Samwise Gamgee, press forward on their quest to destroy a powerful and corrupting object that could bring darkness to the entire world. As they travel deeper into hostile lands, they encounter new allies and face terrifying challenges, both external and internal, that test their friendship and resolve.Meanwhile, Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli continue their pursuit of Merry and Pippin, two hobbits who have been captured by the forces of darkness. Their journey leads them into the heart of Rohan, a kingdom on the brink of war, where they must unite with its people to stand against the rising threat. Amid political turmoil and war, they face powerful foes and the looming shadow of an ancient evil.Tolkien’s storytelling deepens in The Two Towers, focusing on themes of hope in the face of despair, the complexity of leadership, and the enduring power of friendship. The novel explores the consequences of power, sacrifice, and the fight between light and shadow, building toward a climactic struggle that sets the stage for the trilogy’s conclusion.

The Return Of The King

The Return of the King, the final volume of The Lord of the Rings trilogy, brings the long and perilous journey to a breathtaking conclusion. The forces of darkness led by the Dark Lord Sauron are on the verge of overwhelming the free peoples of Middle-earth, and the fate of the world rests on the shoulders of an unlikely group of heroes. As the battle for the survival of all that is good intensifies, the bonds of friendship, loyalty, and courage are tested in ways none could have predicted.Frodo Baggins, burdened with the task of destroying a powerful object that can bring ruin to all, has come to the end of his journey. With his loyal companion, Sam, he faces the final, most perilous stretch of their quest in the shadow of Sauron’s dark influence. Their struggle is not just against enemies, but against the corrupting power of the very object they carry.Meanwhile, Aragorn, the true heir to the throne of Gondor, must step into his role as a leader in the final conflict, uniting scattered forces to defend the world’s last strongholds. With the armies of Middle-earth preparing for war, and alliances being forged in the face of overwhelming odds, the stage is set for a final confrontation that will determine the fate of all.Tolkien’s masterwork reaches its emotional and thrilling climax, exploring themes of hope, sacrifice, redemption, and the enduring power of courage, all against the backdrop of a world at war.

Between Two Fires

Between Two Fires by Christopher Buehlman is a haunting and immersive historical fantasy set during the tumultuous period of the Black Death in 14th-century France. The story follows Thomas, a former knight turned deserter, as he grapples with the horrors of war, plague, and a world in the grip of both human and supernatural forces. After abandoning his post during a battle, Thomas becomes a man on the run, haunted by guilt, and desperate to survive.When he stumbles upon a mysterious and deadly group of travelers, led by a captivating woman named Adeline, he becomes entangled in a dark and twisted conspiracy. Adeline’s group is not merely fleeing from the plague—they are running from something far worse: an ancient evil that preys on the souls of the living. As the world around them falls apart, Thomas and his companions must face terrifying forces—both human and monstrous—that threaten to tear apart the very fabric of existence.Buehlman masterfully blends historical events with dark, supernatural elements, creating a rich, atmospheric narrative. Themes of faith, survival, redemption, and the clash between good and evil run throughout, as Thomas is forced to confront his past and the true nature of the horrors that pursue him. Between Two Fires is a gripping tale that explores the depths of human despair, the fragility of life, and the forces—seen and unseen—that shape history.

Negative Space

Negative Space is a dark, hallucinatory novel that follows several adolescents in a small New England town unnerved by grief, disappearance, and strange, surreal forces. The atmosphere is heavy with dread and ambiguity—there are a series of unexplained deaths, animals behaving oddly, and rituals that may or may not be supernatural or metaphorical. The characters drift through their days in a haze of desperation and longing, each trying to make sense of a world that seems to have slipped off its moorings.Lu is one of the central figures, struggling with identity and isolation, drawn into rituals and uncanny experiences. Jill, Tyler, Ahmir and others orbit around Lu’s story—some seeking escape, others confronting the past, some simply trying not to get swallowed up themselves. Through drug use, dreams, visions, and fragmented reality, the narrative weaves together personal trauma, longing, guilt, and a sense that something cosmic and indifferent is tugging at the edges of everything.Yeager doesn’t offer easy explanations: much is left unresolved, much is grotesque or dreamlike, and the line between what’s real and what’s hallucinated is often intentionally blurred. The style leans hard into mood, sensory detail, discomfort, the uncanny. The book is intense, and reads as much like a psychological or cosmic horror as a coming‑of‑age story—but one where coming of age means learning just how fragile one’s sense of self and safety can be.If you like literature that lingers in ambiguity, that lets dread build without neat closures, Negative Space will carry you into places both beautiful and brutal.

1984

1984 is a chilling and thought-provoking dystopian novel set in a totalitarian society where surveillance, propaganda, and absolute control define every aspect of life. The story follows Winston Smith, a low-ranking member of the ruling Party, as he quietly begins to question the oppressive regime that governs his world. In this society, independent thought is a crime, history is constantly rewritten, and even emotions are subject to regulation.Winston lives under the ever-watchful eye of Big Brother, the Party’s omnipresent leader, whose face is plastered across the city and whose gaze is enforced by the Thought Police. Citizens are expected to conform absolutely, not only in their actions but in their beliefs and even their thoughts. Despite this, Winston harbors doubts about the Party’s version of reality and dreams of truth, freedom, and genuine human connection.As he begins to seek meaning and authenticity in a world that has erased both, Winston finds small ways to rebel—acts that, in his world, carry enormous risk. His journey is both deeply personal and profoundly political, unfolding in a grimly plausible future shaped by absolute power and manipulation.George Orwell’s 1984 is a searing critique of authoritarianism, censorship, and the distortion of truth. Through its bleak yet unforgettable vision, the novel explores themes of identity, resistance, memory, and control. It remains a powerful warning about the dangers of unchecked power—and a haunting reflection on what it means to be free.

Paprika

Paprika is a dazzling and mind-bending science fiction novel that blurs the line between dreams and reality. Set in a near-future Japan, the story centers on Dr. Atsuko Chiba, a brilliant psychiatrist who treats patients using an experimental technology that allows therapists to enter their clients’ dreams. Under the alter-ego “Paprika,” Chiba ventures into these subconscious realms to uncover the roots of mental disturbances in a vivid, surreal dreamscape.But when one of the dream devices is stolen, reality itself begins to unravel. Dreams start bleeding into the waking world, and individuals—including high-ranking officials—are being overtaken by hallucinations and delusions. As the boundary between dream and reality begins to collapse, Dr. Chiba must navigate a rapidly disintegrating world to uncover the culprit behind the chaos and prevent a psychological catastrophe.Yasutaka Tsutsui’s Paprika is a cerebral, fast-paced blend of science fiction, psychological thriller, and speculative mystery. Rich in imagery and filled with bizarre, often playful sequences, it explores the deep and often dangerous power of the unconscious mind. The novel raises provocative questions about identity, perception, and the ethical implications of technology that can manipulate the human psyche.Far more than just a high-concept sci-fi adventure, Paprika is also a philosophical exploration of desire, control, and the fragile structures that define reality. With its dreamlike narrative and razor-sharp intelligence, it remains a landmark work in speculative fiction—and an unforgettable journey through the depths of the mind.

Piranesi

Piranesi is a beautifully strange and atmospheric novel that invites readers into a mysterious, labyrinthine world unlike any other. The story follows a man known only as Piranesi, who lives in a vast, ruined House filled with endless halls, grand statues, and an ocean that rises and falls through its lower levels. He spends his days exploring its wonders, meticulously documenting the tides, tracking the stars through its upper chambers, and caring for the bones of the few dead he finds. To Piranesi, the House is a sacred place—a world unto itself, full of meaning and mystery.Though seemingly alone, Piranesi is visited periodically by another person he calls “the Other,” who enlists him in a search for a secret knowledge said to be hidden somewhere in the House. But as strange messages appear and memories begin to surface, Piranesi starts to question everything he thought he knew about the world—and himself.Susanna Clarke’s Piranesi is a lyrical, dreamlike novel that blends fantasy, mystery, and philosophical reflection into a short yet profound experience. Written in journal entries and meditative prose, it gradually reveals its secrets with quiet elegance. Themes of solitude, memory, identity, and the pursuit of truth run deeply through the narrative.With its haunting beauty and sense of wonder, Piranesi is both a puzzle and a poem—a story about how we make sense of the world, and how the world, in turn, shapes us. It’s a novel best entered without expectations, and best remembered long after it ends.

Into Thin Air

Into Thin Air is a gripping and harrowing non-fiction account of a disastrous expedition to the summit of Mount Everest. Written by journalist and mountaineer Jon Krakauer, the book chronicles his firsthand experience during the 1996 Everest climbing season, which ended in one of the deadliest events in the mountain’s history.Sent by a magazine to report on the growing commercialization of Everest, Krakauer joins a guided expedition led by an experienced climber. What begins as a dream ascent to the world’s highest peak quickly spirals into a life-or-death struggle against extreme altitude, brutal weather, and human limitation. As conditions worsen near the summit, climbers are forced to make impossible decisions—and the mountain proves indifferent to even the best-prepared adventurers.Told with stark honesty, emotional intensity, and journalistic precision, Into Thin Air is more than a survival story. It explores the motivations that drive people to undertake such dangerous feats, the ethical dilemmas faced in extreme environments, and the fine line between ambition and hubris. Krakauer also reflects on his own role in the tragedy, making the narrative deeply personal and morally complex.Through vivid detail and powerful storytelling, the book brings readers into the thin, oxygen-starved world above 26,000 feet, where every step is a struggle and every decision carries enormous consequences.Whether you're fascinated by adventure, survival, or the human psyche under pressure, Into Thin Air is a haunting, unforgettable journey into the heights—and costs—of human ambition.
